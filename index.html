Lichen Zhang

Product designer at Smile.io

Growing up, I wanted to be an engineer. At UWaterloo, I learned the value of applying a quantitative approach to problem-solving to uncover both generalizable and unintuitive truths about the world. However, I couldn't shake the feeling that pursuing a strictly “technical” career would neglect my desire to understand the complexity of people.

Over time, I realized that what I’m best at is working with multidisciplinary teams to seek and then distill information to discover problems at their core.

Some ✨highlights of my work at Smile:

  • 💎 Clarity over simplicity: redesigning how merchants configure their loyalty programs
  • 💳 Bringing Smile to WooCommerce
  • 💸 Identifying, prioritizing, and triaging UX debt
  • 🏗 Contributing to our Polaris-based design system on Figma

My research and entrepreneurial endeavours have had the greatest influence on how I think through and approach problems. For a period of time, I also worked on my own startup, 🏥PriveHealth, a cybersecurity training platform designed for healthcare professionals.

I’ve spoken on the topic of design on campus, shared my research multi-nationally, and pitched to investors. These opportunities have allowed me to share the stage with curious audiences and meet wonderful people that teach me more about my profession and myself.
